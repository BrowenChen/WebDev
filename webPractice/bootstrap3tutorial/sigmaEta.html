What We Do

Sigma Eta Pi is dedicated to developing human potential. Our mission is to transform individuals into starters who follow their passion, develop lasting relationships, and create value through innovative pursuits.

We educate our brothers with the most comprehensive knowledge of the start-up ecosystem and venture processes and equip them with a concrete set of skills sourced from the diverse backgrounds of our house.

"Sigma Eta Pi is a toolbox; it enables us to build not on our next big startup, but on ourselves. SEP gave me the confidence and skill set to pursue any future venture in my life."
Kirtan Upadhyaya, Alpha Class
"SEP was a time of exploration, an opportunity for growth, and most importantly, a place to call home. New ways of thinking, seeing, and believing come together; the world you used to know becomes much smaller, and the world you didn't know becomes much brighter."
Andy Yang, Founding Member
Sigma Eta Pi is filled with individuals that I can confidently say are my inspiration and my family. We're a little quirky, a lot of fun, and undeniably passionate and talented. I grew immeasurably as a leader and entrepreneur and met my best friends. Hands down the best and most defining part of my college experience.
Eric Liu, Founding Member

Events

Below are some of our past events. Sigma Eta Pi runs one professional event per semester.

  • The Social Media Effect
    Fall 2013

    The Social Media Effect was a speaker panel featuring Spotify, Mounza, Ribbon, and other prominent startups in the Bay Area. This panel of industry experts discussed social media, and it's impact on the start-up landscape both in the present and the future.

  • The Starter Fair
    Spring 2013

    The Starter Fair brought together unique entrepreneurs and startups to showcase their projects to the Berkeley community. By exploring and critiquing products popularized on crowdsourcing platforms such as Kickstarter and Indiegogo, both entrepreneurs and students enjoyed an afternoon of creative discovery.

  • Table Talks
    Fall 2012

    Table Talks provided opportunities for students interested in start-ups to engage with experienced entrepreneurs. Twelve entrepreneurs from various fields attended as speakers and discussed strategies, anecdotes, and the necessary steps in order to maximize success as a start-up.

  • The Pitch
    Spring 2012

    During The Pitch, five student-initiated start-ups pitched their concepts to eight venture capitalists from firms such as General Catalyst, Lowenstein Sandler, and Javelin Venture Partners. More than 150 people attended and enjoyed learning from the presentations, responses, and critique.

  • Cal Entrepreneurship Expo
    Fall 2011

    The Cal Entrepreneurship Expo assembled an array of speakers and startups in a unique event that drew over 300 attendees. Speeches were given by Steve Blank, Justin Yoshimura of 500Friends, and Tom Yedwab of Khan Academy. The event also included a speaker panel of successful entrepreneurs, over a dozen start-up booths, and a productive networking environment.
